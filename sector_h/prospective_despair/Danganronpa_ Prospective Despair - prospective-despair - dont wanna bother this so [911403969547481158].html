angie the epic gamer 19-Nov-21 06:54 PM
"Are you- Are you kidding me...?" She walked out of the elevator, standing on the cold, snow floor. She did not want this to be the case. She did not want this to be the outcome of all of this. Why would she want the outcome... that would mean that all she's done these few years has gone to waste? She shivered, staring at the rubble which was once a large city in the past. Everything. All the people living here, the homes, the stores, the apartments... was gone. The final blow was that two people that might be gone were the two people she was trying to look for her whole life. How was she supposed to take this in? She didn't know how she was going to process this information all at once. She just wanted to ignore the voices around her to take this all in. "The city... there were people here, and everyone's gone now..." She sighed, closing her eyes. "And my parents are probably dead too, aren't they? I don't even know where they are anymore... they could be anywhere..." And that's what she did. Ignoring all the voices around her, she walked farther away from the elevator and into the cold and winter wasteland... it would be wrong to say wonderland, considering the state of the area and possibly a lot of other places in the world. "I-I should've just given up... Why was I so desperate to find some people that were probably dead this whole time? Why did I feel the need to become an ultimate in the first place?" What she said would be possibly unheard by everyone who chose to stay around, as she spoke as quietly as she could to avoid being heard. As quiet as a mouse. She stood as still as a statue, thinking to herself. Oh, how clueless she was. Asking herself all these questions that no one, including herself, knew the answer to. A few reasons came to her mind, yet quickly disappeared as Miki thought of a reason why that wouldn’t be the case.
... She became an ultimate because that’s all what she wanted to be? Nope. She wanted to be the Ultimate Pianist, not some person who isn’t skilled in any other area except for picking locks and opening doors. ‘They’, who were now exposed as kidnappers due to the information on her file, wouldn’t want her to become an ultimate anyway. In fact, that’s what ‘they’ would hope to avoid. Yet, she still was allowed to attend Hope’s Peak. All because of a promise. She knew the reason why she wanted to go to Hope’s Peak, and it wasn’t to achieve her dreams. It was to hopefully become more known, so she would have a chance to see her parents again. But they were quite possibly dead. Dead beforehand or dead from… this. Trying to do whatever she was asked, getting blamed for all the things she didn’t do… for what? Hope that she would see her parents again at some point? Pfft… looking at all this now, if she were to say that to someone she would look like a joke. A sad joke that wasn’t funny. After some awkward silence, she spoke once again. “... Alright. I get it now.” She sighed, looking up at the sky above. “So this is what some dumb war can do…” They said, finally taking some time to calm down. Her voice wasn’t as quiet now, but she was still speaking at a volume that made it clear she was talking to herself. Rubble. Destruction. Those are the only two words that could describe the world as it’s now known. How were the surviving ultimates going to live in these conditions? What was the whole point of escaping anymore if everyone they knew was potentially dead? Miki’s parents are gone… they’ve been gone for a while, and now they’re possibly not coming back.
’Them’... well… she did not want to see them again. In fact, she secretly hoped that some bomb dropped on them and they got what they deserved. Good thing is that she probably won’t die once they get out of here… mainly because there was no dangerous place that she’s supposed to call home to go back to. That’s probably the only thing she’s looking forward to, though. She thought that she could escape and manage to live her own life somehow, but the future didn’t seem so bright. It seemed that there was no future at all. If she managed to escape, she would probably have to stay in some other place her whole life just to stay safe from all the warfare. But… that’s better than nothing at all. Maybe now she would have some sort of freedom to live her life as she wanted. A life that she was waiting for years but never came until maybe now. She couldn’t smile now, no. Not while being in front of some rubble. And… she still had her parents to worry about. Her parents being dead was the most of her worries. “... Sorry.” She apologised, still looking at the sky. Who was she apologising to? Her parents? ‘Them’? No… definitely not ‘them’. Maybe herself? That was all up to question. Sometimes Miki doesn’t even know why she’s apologising for things at times, simply becoming a habit of hers. Miki Sunada, the Ultimate Picklock, took one final deep breath before walking back onto the elevator. She had accepted it. She’s seen enough. She’ll just have to worry about herself, and herself only. After all these events, trusting everyone was still difficult for her.
